Call for CHAPTERS

 

for the book entitled

"Bioenergetics, the Immune System, and Bioinformatics"

edited by Newell / Cios / Lightner

to be published by

Springer-Verlag series "Studies in Computational Intelligence”

 

 

We are pleased to request chapters related to bioenergetics, the immune system, and bioinformatics. 

Areas of interest for the issue include electrophysiology, biophysics, computational immunology,

the impact of metabolism on immune function, heat transfer in biological systems, metabolism

and bioinformatics.

Topics of interest in bioenergetics include but are not limited to:

  • Cellular energy choices:  glycolysis and respiration
  • Glucose utilization
  • Fatty acid metabolism
  • Biophysics and electromagnetic radiation
  • Ion and Voltage gated channels
  • Calcium and calcium signaling

Topics of interest in the immune system include but are not limited to:

  • T-Cell Receptor Signaling
  • Cell Death and Apoptosis in the immune system
  • Impact of T cell Metabolism on T cell function
  • Cytokines and immune regulation
  • Impact of Target Cell Metabolism on Autoimmune Diseases
  • The immune system and heart disease

Topics of interests in bioinformatics include but are not limited to:

  • Genomics
    • Structural, signal processing, pattern recognition and data mining
    • Techniques for functional genomics
  • Information management
    • Genomics and proteomics data
  • Modeling
    • Cellular metabolism and inter-cellular signaling
    • Mathematical techniques for immunology modeling and discovery
    • Modeling and simulating receptor and regulatory networks
  • Systems
    • Large-scale microarray gene expression data analysis recognition/classification
    • Hardware/Software systems for genome and proteome-scale modeling
    • Nanotechnology techniques
  • Proteomics
    • Protein interaction with cellular components
    • Comparative, structural and quantitative proteomics
  • Post-translational modification analysis

 

Preferred chapters will be those demonstrating the power behind integrating disciplines in solving

biomedical problems. In addition, because of the breadth of the book, chapters should be written

in a tutorial-like style so that readers with a wide variety of backgrounds will be able to comprehend

the importance of integrating disciplines.

 

We strongly suggest that before submitting a chapter you contact us via email with a 1-page

abstract of the proposed chapter so that we can determine if there is a good match with what

we have in mind. Once your contribution is initially accepted (subject to final acceptance based

on two reviews) you will be asked to follow author guidelines in preparing the chapter, and use

the attached template.

 

Enquiries should be emailed to any of the editors but submission of your 5000 word chapter needs

to be emailed to Krys.Cios@cudenver.edu, by April 1, 2006.
